# **Trader Performance vs Market Sentiment \- Summary**

# **Methodology**

This project analyzes the relationship between market sentiment (Fear vs Greed) and trader performance using historical trading data.

* Cleaned and preprocessed two datasets: sentiment data and trader activity data  
* Converted timestamps and merged datasets based on date  
* Engineered key features such as:  
  * Daily PnL  
  * Trade frequency (number of trades per day)  
  * Trade size (USD)  
  * Long/Short behavior  
* Performed exploratory data analysis and visualizations to compare behavior across sentiment conditions  
* Segmented traders based on behavior (trade size, frequency, and profitability)

**Key Insights**

1. **Market sentiment significantly impacts trader performance**  
   * Higher volatility in PnL is observed during Fear periods, indicating increased uncertainty and risk.  
2. **Traders behave more aggressively during Greed periods**  
   * Increased trade sizes and a higher proportion of long positions suggest bullish and risk-taking behavior.  
3. **Overtrading leads to inconsistent performance**  
   * High-frequency traders tend to show unstable returns compared to moderate-frequency traders.  
4. **Consistent traders outperform aggressive traders**  
   * Traders using controlled position sizes and disciplined strategies achieve more stable outcomes.

**Strategy Recommendations**

1. **Reduce risk during Fear periods**  
   * Traders should lower trade sizes and avoid high-frequency trading during uncertain market conditions.  
2. **Avoid overconfidence during Greed periods**  
   * Limit excessive trading and maintain disciplined strategies even in bullish markets.  
3. **Adopt consistent trading behavior**  
   * Moderate trade sizes and controlled frequency lead to more stable and reliable performance.

**Conclusion**

Market sentiment plays a crucial role in influencing trader decisions and outcomes.  
By adapting trading strategies based on sentiment and maintaining disciplined behavior, traders can reduce risk and improve long-term performance.
